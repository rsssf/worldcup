Uruguay, July 13-30. All games played in Montevideo. Decided in Amsterdam 28 May
1928. Entries deadline 19 Jan 1930, moved to late May (all 13 teams that entered
were qualified). Groups drawn 7 Jul 1930. Semifinals drawn 22 Jul 1930.

== Final  ‹§final›

30.07.30  (15.30) Montevideo, Centenario

URU - ARG 4:2 (1:2)

(-80000) Langenus BEL, Christophe BEL, Saucedo BOL

URU: Ballestero - Mascheroni, Nasazzi (c) - Andrade, Fernández, Gestido -
Dorado, Scarone, Castro, Cea, Iriarte.
ARG: Botasso - Della Torre, Paternoster - J.Evaristo, Monti, Arico
Suárez - Peucelle, Varallo, Stábile, Ferreira (c), M.Evaristo.

1;0 Dorado 12, 1:1 Peucelle 20, 1:2 Stábile 38, 2:2 Cea 58, 3:2 Iriarte
68, 4:2 Castro 89


No match for third place; in contemporary newspapers appearing in Yugoslavia early
September it is claimed Yugoslavia were awarded the third place for having lost to
the eventual winners rather than the runners-up, supposedly in accordance with
Olympic regulations (a rather curious statement as the Olympic football tournament
always had featured a bronze medal match except in ‹1920, see page ../tableso/ol1920f-det› when the set-up was rather
more involved); no official stance of FIFA is known on this - in 1984 an official
FIFA report claimed Yugoslavia beat the USA 3-1 in a match for third place but that
is nonsense (no official match between the two countries, friendly or otherwise, was
played before the ‹1956 Olympic Games, see page ../tableso/ol1956f›); according to the rules applied in later World
Cup tournaments (comparing the overall records of teams eliminated at the same stage),
the USA would be third (for having conceded one goal less in their three matches).
